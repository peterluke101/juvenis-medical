## Content Formula Per Blog Post

### Blog post publishes →

**TikTok (Day 1):**
- Format: 30-60 second text-overlay video OR Dr. Paul talking to camera (preferred)
- Hook (first 3 seconds): symptom-first — "If you're exhausted by 2pm every day, this might be why…"
- Body: 3-4 key points from the blog
- CTA: "Book a free consult — link in bio" (TikTok forces bio link)
- Update TikTok bio link to direct Zoho form URL on posting day
- Women's content angle: perimenopause, weight loss, hormones, energy
- Men's content angle: testosterone, fatigue, performance, muscle loss

**Instagram Post 1 (Day 1):**
- Format: Graphic card (stat, symptom list, or bold claim)
- Caption: 150-200 words, education-first, soft CTA at end
- CTA: Direct Zoho link in caption

**Instagram Post 2 (Day 4-5):**
- Format: Caption-heavy, conversational
- Angle: Personal/relatable ("If you're waking up tired every morning despite 8 hours of sleep…")
- CTA: Direct Zoho link

**Facebook Post 1 (Day 1):**
- Format: Blog share with 3-sentence hook
- Hook pulls the most alarming or relatable stat from the article
- CTA: Direct Zoho link

**Facebook Post 2 (Day 5-6):**
- Format: Standalone tip or question post
- Women's example: "Ladies — are your hormones making it impossible to lose weight no matter what you try?"
- Men's example: "Quick question for the guys in Fort Lauderdale — have you had your testosterone checked in the last 2 years?"
- CTA: Direct Zoho link

**Google Business Post (Day 1-2):**
- 150-300 words, local angle ("Fort Lauderdale women…" / "South Florida men…")
- Links to blog post
- Category: What's New or Offer

## Copy Rules

- Always write for the patient, not the doctor
- Lead with symptoms, not treatments ("exhausted by 2pm?" not "hormone therapy available")
- Women's copy: empathetic, validating — "it's not in your head"
- Men's copy: direct, solution-focused — "here's what's actually happening"
- Use Fort Lauderdale / South Florida geographic anchors for local SEO
- Never make dosing claims or promise specific outcomes — run through `/medical-wording` before posting
- Write as Dr. Paul's voice ("At my practice…") or third person ("Dr. Paul works with…")
- Every post ends with a direct action: book, call, or click
